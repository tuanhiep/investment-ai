Title: Margin of Safety
Summary: Margin of Safety is the central concept of Graham's philosophy. It protects the investor against errors in judgment, weak assumptions, and market volatility by requiring a meaningful discount to conservative intrinsic value.
Quote: The function of the margin of safety is to make an accurate estimate of the future less necessary.
Keywords: risk, valuation buffer, defensive, intrinsic value
Khi: Tinh - Phong Thu - Khong Vi

Title: Intrinsic Value
Summary: Intrinsic value is not a precise number. It is a reasoned range built from earnings power, assets, balance-sheet quality, cyclicality, and conservative assumptions.
Quote: An investment operation is one which, upon thorough analysis, promises safety of principal and an adequate return.
Keywords: valuation, earnings power, assets, conservative assumptions
Khi: Tinh - Phan Tich - Ky Luat

Title: Defensive Investor
Summary: A defensive investor should prefer simplicity, diversification, strong finances, long records of profitability, and valuation discipline. The goal is durable adequacy, not excitement.
Quote: The investor's chief problem, and even his worst enemy, is likely to be himself.
Keywords: defensive investor, diversification, profitability, temperament
Khi: On Dinh - Khiem Ton - Ben Bi

Title: Enterprising Investor
Summary: An enterprising investor may search for special situations or undervalued securities, but only with deeper work, better evidence, and stricter risk control.
Quote: To achieve satisfactory investment results is easier than most people realize; to achieve superior results is harder than it looks.
Keywords: enterprising investor, special situations, research, risk control
Khi: Chu Dong - Nghien Cuu - Kiem Soat

Title: Mr Market
Summary: Market prices are offers, not instructions. The disciplined investor uses volatility as a servant and refuses to let changing quotations define business value.
Quote: The market is there to serve you, not to instruct you.
Keywords: market volatility, behavior, discipline, long term
Khi: Binh Than - Doc Lap - Khong FOMO

Title: Earnings Stability
Summary: Graham-style analysis values durable earnings more than dramatic narratives. A long record of positive earnings and manageable debt deserves more trust than optimistic projections.
Quote: Operations for profit should be based not on optimism but on arithmetic.
Keywords: earnings, stability, debt, profitability, arithmetic
Khi: Thuc Chung - Can Trong - Ro Rang
