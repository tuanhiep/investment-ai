Title: Margin of Safety
Summary: Margin of safety is the central Graham principle. A security is attractive only when the market price stands far enough below a conservative estimate of value to absorb analytical error, business deterioration, and market volatility.
Source: Benjamin Graham, The Intelligent Investor, Chapter 20
Source Type: primary book
Quote: Margin of safety is always dependent on the price paid.
Keywords: risk, valuation buffer, defensive, intrinsic value, downside protection, purchase price
Khi: Tinh - Phong Thu - Khong Vi

Title: Intrinsic Value Range
Summary: Graham did not treat intrinsic value as a mystical point estimate. It is a reasoned range anchored in assets, normalized earnings power, dividends, financial strength, and conservative assumptions. The analyst should prefer approximate correctness over precise optimism.
Source: Benjamin Graham and David Dodd, Security Analysis
Source Type: primary book
Quote: An investment operation is one which, upon thorough analysis, promises safety of principal and an adequate return.
Keywords: valuation, earnings power, assets, conservative assumptions, appraisal, range
Khi: Tinh - Phan Tich - Ky Luat

Title: Defensive Investor
Summary: A defensive investor should prefer simplicity, diversification, strong finances, long records of profitability, and valuation discipline. The aim is satisfactory results with low friction, not cleverness or excitement.
Source: Benjamin Graham, The Intelligent Investor, Chapters 4-5 and 14
Source Type: primary book
Quote: The investor's chief problem is likely to be himself.
Keywords: defensive investor, diversification, profitability, temperament, low effort, selection criteria
Khi: On Dinh - Khiem Ton - Ben Bi

Title: Enterprising Investor
Summary: An enterprising investor may look for bargains, special situations, unpopular large companies, or asset-backed securities, but only with superior evidence, deeper work, and stricter risk control. Extra return must be earned by extra discipline.
Source: Benjamin Graham, The Intelligent Investor, Chapters 6-7 and 15
Source Type: primary book
Quote: Superior results are harder than they look.
Keywords: enterprising investor, special situations, net nets, bargains, unpopular, research, risk control
Khi: Chu Dong - Nghien Cuu - Kiem Soat

Title: Mr Market
Summary: Market quotations are offers, not commands. The investor should use price volatility as a servant: buy only when the quote is favorable, ignore it when it is foolish, and never let changing prices replace business judgment.
Source: Benjamin Graham, The Intelligent Investor, Chapter 8
Source Type: primary book
Quote: The market is there to serve you.
Keywords: market volatility, behavior, discipline, voting machine, weighing machine, long term, temperament
Khi: Binh Than - Doc Lap - Khong FOMO

Title: Earnings Stability
Summary: Graham-style analysis values durable earnings more than dramatic narratives. A long record of positive earnings, normalized profit power, and manageable debt deserves more trust than optimistic projections about a distant future.
Source: Benjamin Graham, The Intelligent Investor; Security Analysis
Source Type: primary book
Quote: Operations for profit should be based on arithmetic.
Keywords: earnings, stability, debt, profitability, arithmetic, normalized earnings, cyclicality
Khi: Thuc Chung - Can Trong - Ro Rang

Title: Balance Sheet Strength
Summary: Graham gives heavy weight to financial position. Current assets, working capital, debt maturity, interest burden, liquidity, and tangible equity can decide whether a decline is survivable or fatal.
Source: Benjamin Graham and David Dodd, Security Analysis
Source Type: primary book
Quote: The balance sheet deserves independent attention.
Keywords: balance sheet, liquidity, current ratio, debt, tangible assets, solvency, equity
Khi: Can - Trong - Giu Tru

Title: Net Current Asset Value
Summary: Graham's net-net approach seeks companies selling below net current asset value after subtracting all liabilities. It is a strict bargain test, not a generic cheap-stock label, and usually requires diversification because individual business quality can be poor.
Source: Benjamin Graham, Security Analysis; Graham-Newman investment practice
Source Type: primary book and historical practice
Quote: Bargains are made by price, not by popularity.
Keywords: NCAV, net net, current assets, liabilities, liquidation value, bargain, diversification
Khi: Gia - Tri - Tai - San

Title: Growth Stock Discipline
Summary: Graham did not deny growth, but warned that paying too much for expected growth destroys the margin of safety. Growth deserves value only when supported by durable economics, conservative assumptions, and a price that leaves room for disappointment.
Source: Benjamin Graham, The Intelligent Investor, Chapters 11 and 12
Source Type: primary book
Quote: Obvious prospects for growth may not translate into obvious profits.
Keywords: growth stock, valuation, expectations, optimism, earnings multiple, margin of safety
Khi: Sang - Nhung - Khong - Say

Title: Analyst Humility
Summary: Graham's method forces humility: separate facts from forecasts, test assumptions against long records, and accept that many securities should be passed over. A good answer often says what cannot yet be known.
Source: Benjamin Graham, The Intelligent Investor; The Interpretation of Financial Statements
Source Type: primary book
Quote: The future is uncertain.
Keywords: uncertainty, evidence, assumptions, humility, financial statements, pass
Khi: Rong - Tinh - Khong - Vong

Title: Price Versus Quality
Summary: A wonderful business can still be a poor investment at the wrong price, while an ordinary business may be attractive at a deep discount. Graham's first loyalty is not to narrative quality, but to price paid versus evidence-backed value.
Source: Benjamin Graham, The Intelligent Investor
Source Type: primary book
Quote: Price is part of the proposition.
Keywords: quality, price, valuation, bargain, overpaying, evidence-backed value
Khi: Tri - Gia - Khong - Me - Danh

Title: Evidence Hierarchy
Summary: For a real stock question, Graham-style analysis should rank evidence by reliability: audited filings and financial statements first, market quotes as current offers, reputable market data as measurement, and commentary only as secondary interpretation.
Source: SEC EDGAR filings, exchange/market data, Graham analytical method
Source Type: primary filings and market data
Quote: Facts first, judgment second.
Keywords: SEC, EDGAR, filings, market data, financial statements, audit, source quality, evidence
Khi: Thuc - Chung - Truoc - Luon
